Welcome to our Digital Safety and Security Page

Digital safety and security are important aspects of our online lives. This page is made to help you with essential information on how to be more secure in the digital world.

Best Practices for Digital Safety and Security

1. Set up a Secure Digital Environment and Safeguard Your Workspace

Create a secure digital environment by:

  • Using strong, unique passwords for all accounts.
  • Implementing multi-factor authentication for an extra layer of security.
  • Securing your physical workspace to prevent unauthorized access.

2. Identify and Address Digital Security Weaknesses

Be proactive in addressing security weaknesses by:

  • Regularly updating software to patch vulnerabilities.
  • Educating yourself and your team about phishing risks and how to recognize them.
  • Reviewing and adjusting software permissions to limit potential risks.

3. Back Up Your Data Safely

Implement a robust data backup strategy by:

  • Regularly backing up critical data to an external and secure location.
  • Testing your backup and recovery processes to ensure they work effectively.
  • Choosing a backup strategy that meets the specific needs of your network.

4. Evaluate Your Risk and Choose Security Tools

Assess your risk for cyberattacks and enhance your security with:

  • Conducting regular risk assessments to identify potential vulnerabilities.
  • Choosing and implementing security tools that align with your organization's needs.
  • Monitoring and updating your security measures as the threat landscape evolves.

5. Maintain and Update Hardware and Software

Prevent cyberattacks and data loss risks by:

  • Regularly updating operating systems, antivirus software, and other security tools.
  • Replacing outdated hardware that may pose security risks.
  • Implementing policies for secure software development and usage.

6. Set Up a Response Strategy for Digital Worst-Case Scenarios

Prepare for digital worst-case scenarios by:

  • Developing an incident response plan to address cybersecurity incidents promptly.
  • Training your team on the response strategy and conducting regular drills.
  • Establishing communication channels for reporting and responding to incidents.
